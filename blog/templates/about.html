5G (fifth generation)

5G (fifth generation) is the latest generation of mobile network technology, designed to provide faster data speeds, lower latency, and higher capacity than previous generations. One of the key technologies used in 5G networks is Massive MIMO. Massive MIMO (Multiple-Input Multiple-Output) is a technology that uses a large number of antennas at the base station to improve the performance and capacity of a wireless network. The basic idea behind Massive MIMO is to use many antennas to transmit and receive data simultaneously on the same frequency, in order to increase the signal quality and reduce interference. In Massive MIMO, the base station is equipped with a large number of antennas, typically 64 or more, that are used to communicate with multiple users simultaneously.
